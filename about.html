About Project Gutenberg

To encourage the creation and distribution of eBooks.

The History of a Library

Project Gutenberg was the first provider of free electronic books, or eBooks. Michael Hart, founder of Project Gutenberg, invented eBooks in 1971 and his memory continues to inspire the creation of eBooks and related content today.


Our mission is simple: to encourage the creation and distribution of eBooks. We are fueled by ideas, ideals, and by an idealistic core of volunteers. Project Gutenberg is powered by ideas rather than by financial or political power.


Most of the items in our collection are the full texts of public domain books. You can read them here online, or download them for offline reading. No fee or registration is required.
